Storage Efficiency Comparison

ANONs (Bitcoin Stamps): Uses on-chain layered storage for all images. Approximately ~6 MB total on-chain data for 10k avatars (all traits stored once).

Fully On-Chain ETH: Storing each image on Ethereum directly could be hundreds of MB for 10k NFTs (prohibitively expensive in gas).

IPFS-Based ETH: Only pointers on-chain (small bytes); bulk image data off-chain on IPFS (not permanent unless maintained).

Recursion & Compression

ANONs are powered by the SRC-721 protocol on Bitcoin. Instead of storing each image separately, traits are stored once on-chain and reused via references. Every ANON is defined by a compact JSON that assembles these trait layers. This **compression** slashes storage needs and minting costs while keeping all data immutable on Bitcoin.

**Recursion:** The next evolution ‚Äì fully recursive minting. This means an ANON can call upon on-chain data (like trait files or even code) during its creation. Imagine generative art logic on-chain, or a 10k collection minted by only referencing existing pieces. Recursion opens the door to on-chain randomness and complex compositions without adding data bloat.

In practice, a recursive ANON mint might only use 2 outputs to create a randomized avatar, reducing fees by ~30%. The result: ANONs achieve Ethereum-like complexity and ArtBlocks-style generative art, but entirely on Bitcoin, **forever**. High-res art, low cost, zero off-chain dependencies ‚Äì that's the power of recursion and compression in ANONs.
